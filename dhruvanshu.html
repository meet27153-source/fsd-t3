The Indian Cricket Team, known as the "Men in Blue," is a global powerhouse, governed by the BCCI, renowned for its skilled players like Tendulkar, Kohli, and Dhoni, who led them to multiple ICC trophies including the 1983 & 2011 World Cups and 2007 T20 World Cup. Emerging from early struggles to become consistent contenders, India's strength lies in its strong domestic structure, adaptable strategy, and passionate fan base, making them a symbol of national pride and a dominant force across Test, ODI, and T20 formats, consistently producing world-class talent and inspiring millions. The team has played 598 Test matches, winning 185, losing 188, with 224 draws and 1 tie. As of August 2025, India is ranked fourth in the ICC Men's Test Team Rankings with 107 rating points. India have played in two of the three World Test Championship finals, finishing runners-up in 2021 and 2023. The team has played 1,071 ODI matches, winning 569, losing 448, tying 10 and with 44 ending in a no-result. As of September 2025, India is ranked first in the ICC Men's ODI Team Rankings with 124 rating points. India have appeared in the World Cup final four times and have won the title twice. India have also won the Champions Trophy three times. The team has played 259 Twenty20 International matches, winning 172, losing 72, tying 7 and with 8 ending in a no-result.
